Module 01

What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted entities — banks, companies, or even coworkers — to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or login credentials.

📧

Email Phishing

Fake emails mimicking banks, PayPal, or IT departments to steal credentials.

📱

Smishing (SMS)

Text messages with malicious links disguised as package delivery or OTP alerts.

📞

Vishing (Voice)

Phone calls pretending to be tech support or bank fraud departments.

🎯

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks personalized with your name, company, or role.

🚨
Phishing accounts for over 90% of all data breaches. It is the #1 entry point for ransomware and corporate espionage worldwide.
Module 02

Spot a Phishing Email

Study this fake email. Every red flag is real — attackers use these exact techniques.

From: security-alerts@paypa1-support.com ⚠
To: you@example.com
Subject: ⚠ URGENT: Your account has been SUSPENDED

Dear Valued Customer,

We have detected unusual activity on your PayPal account. Your account has been temporarily suspended for security reasons.

To restore access, you must verify your identity within 24 hours or your account will be permanently closed.

Click here to verify now:
http://paypa1-secure-verify.ru/login?id=839201

Failure to verify will result in permanent account closure and loss of funds.

Regards,
PayPal Security Team

🚩 Red Flags Identified

🔴 Fake domain: "paypa1-support.com" — '1' instead of 'l'. This is typosquatting.
🔴 Urgency + threats: "24 hours", "permanent closure" — pressure tactics to disable your judgment.
🔴 Suspicious link: ".ru" domain, random ID — not PayPal's real domain (paypal.com).
🔴 Generic greeting: "Valued Customer" — real companies use your actual name.
🔴 Fear & loss framing: "loss of funds" — emotional manipulation to make you act without thinking.
Module 03

Social Engineering Tactics

Phishing isn't just technical — it exploits human psychology. Click each tactic to learn how attackers manipulate you.

⏰ Urgency & Scarcity ▼
Creates time pressure so you don't think critically. Examples: "Your account expires in 2 hours!", "Last chance to claim your reward." Always pause and verify independently when you feel rushed.
👔 Authority Impersonation ▼
Pretends to be your CEO, IT department, bank, or government. People are conditioned to comply with authority figures. Real organizations never ask for passwords via email.
😨 Fear & Consequences ▼
Threatens account suspension, legal action, or financial loss. Fear bypasses rational thinking. If an email makes you feel afraid, that's a major red flag — legitimate orgs rarely threaten.
🎁 Reward & Greed ▼
Offers prizes, refunds, or unexpected windfalls. "You've won $500!" or "Unclaimed tax refund." If it sounds too good to be true — it is. Always verify through official channels.
🤝 Pretexting ▼
Attacker creates a fictional scenario to extract info. E.g., "I'm from IT, we need your password to fix a server issue." Legit IT staff NEVER need your password. Zero exceptions.
Module 04

How to Stay Protected

Follow these practices to defend yourself against phishing attacks:

  • 01Verify the sender domain carefully. "paypa1.com" is not "paypal.com". Hover over links before clicking to see the real URL in your browser's status bar.
  • 02Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all accounts. Even if attackers steal your password, MFA blocks them from logging in.
  • 03Never enter credentials via email links. Open your browser manually and type the official site address. Bookmark important sites.
  • 04When in doubt, call to verify. If your "bank" emails you about suspicious activity, hang up and call the number on the back of your card.
  • 05Use a password manager. It won't autofill credentials on fake sites because the domain doesn't match — a built-in phishing defense.
  • 06Keep software updated. Browser and OS updates patch vulnerabilities that malicious sites can exploit even without you clicking anything.
  • 07Report suspicious emails. Use your email client's "Report Phishing" button. This helps protect everyone in your organization.
  • 08Think before you click. The most powerful defense is a 5-second pause to ask: "Was I expecting this? Does this make sense?"
✅
Remember: No legitimate organization will ever ask for your password, OTP, or full card details via email or phone. Ever.
